Shot Analytics

Jump to Shot Maps

Analytics have become increasingly important in many sports today; the same is true for the sport of ice hockey. One aspect of the game that is commonly studied is the spatial location of a player shooting a puck at a goaltender. This type of analysis aims to answer a few questions.

  1. Is there an "optimal shot location" for every player?
    • Is this true regardless of player handedness?
    • Is it true regardless of the player's assigned position?
  2. Do some players tend to miss shots from a specific location?
    • Is their success random in relation to location?
    • Are there trends of locations from which more players take shots?
  3. We know that cycling can be done to get into good position. Where is that position?
    • Our analysis here may not answer this, but can provide some good insight


In order to gain some intuition as to how shot location varies, we begin with exploratory data analysis (EDA) using a scatterplot overlay on an ice rink.

How to Use Shot Location Info

Cycling is a practice used by forward lines in order to maintain posession of the puck while in the offensive zone. It is a way of keeping the puck while forwards move into higher scoring percentage regions. This following video explains how cycling works. If a player know where they will have the most success on a shot, they can cylce with their linemates until one of them is able to get a shot off at that location.
